Return on Equity (ROE)

Welcome to FinAyush Finance Insights.

What is Return on Equity (ROE)?

Return on Equity (ROE) is a profitability ratio that measures how efficiently a company generates profit from the capital invested by its shareholders.

ROE Formula

ROE = (Net Profit / Shareholders' Equity) × 100

Why is ROE Important?

ROE tells us how much profit a company earns for every ₹1 invested by its shareholders.

Example

Company A
Net Profit = ₹20 Crore
Shareholders' Equity = ₹100 Crore
ROE = (20 / 100) × 100 = 20%

Company B
Net Profit = ₹30 Crore
Shareholders' Equity = ₹300 Crore
ROE = (30 / 300) × 100 = 10%

Company A is more efficient in using shareholders' funds because it generates a higher ROE.

Is a High ROE Always Good?

No. A very high ROE is not always a good sign. Sometimes a company may have a high ROE because it has taken excessive debt or because shareholders' equity is very low. Therefore, ROE should always be analyzed along with other financial ratios such as Debt-to-Equity Ratio, ROA, and Profit Margin.

Industries with High ROE

Asset-light industries such as IT, Software, Consulting, and Financial Services generally have higher ROE because they require relatively less capital to generate profits.

Capital-intensive industries such as Steel, Power, and Telecom generally have lower ROE because they require significant investment in assets and infrastructure.

FinAyush Insight

Never judge a company only by its ROE. Always compare it with industry peers and analyze it along with ROA, ROCE, Debt-to-Equity Ratio, and Profit Margin before making an investment decision.
